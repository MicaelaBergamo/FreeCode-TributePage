Soda Stereo

The first hispanic group to achieve mainstream success in Latin America



One of the biggest Latin American rock bands

Soda Stereo is one of the most important and influent bands in all of Argentinian and Latin America rock history, with a massive popularity on the entire continent from mid 80's to the present day.

First with pop influences, then dark and funk, Madchester and trip-hop, alternative rock and electronic music, even revisiting old Argentine and international rock, always with avant-garde musical elements mixed with classic rock, the result is unique and original. A tremendously personal combination that marked three generations of fans and musicians. Gustavo Cerati, Zeta Bosio and Charly Alberti bring new songs, clothes, haircuts, ways of composing, playing and singing to rock music.


Here's a timeline about Soda Stereo history

  • (1982) The first steps
    Soda Stereo was born with the union of Gustavo Cerati, Zeta Bosio and Charly Alberti, three twentysomethings who sought to put together a group with influences from the new new-wave style from England and the United States, not very explored in Argentina.

  • (1984) The debut album
    Soda finally manages to record his debut album, titled “Soda Stereo”. You could hear the songs constantly on the radio, the press celebrated them as the revelation group of the year, and the recitals multiplied in the nightclubs of the suburbs.

  • (1989) International Glory
    They give more than 30 shows in Mexican cities, in addition to Panama, Costa Rica, Guatemala and Honduras. They close the tour with two recitals at the legendary Hollywood Palace theater in Los Angeles. At that point, Soda already exceeds the first million plates sold worldwide.

  • (1991) Av. Nueve de Julio
    They set off on an international tour that starts in Venezuela and continues through Mexico and the United States.They return to their country and receive the proposal to give a show in the middle of Nueve de Julio Avenue. This historic recital brings together 350 thousand people who occupy more than twenty blocks of extension.

  • (1993) Solo Projects
    After spending a decade playing almost non-stop, Soda Stereo decides to stop for a while. Gustavo Cerati records his first solo work.Zeta is dedicated to the artistic production of renowned artists, and Charly Alberti releases an album of alternative and avant-garde rock with the duo Plum.

  • (1997) The Farewell
    In May 1997, in the best musical and successful moment of Soda, the separation is suddenly announced and the realization of a short farewell tour through stadiums in Mexico, Venezuela, Chile and Argentina

  • (2007) Me Verás Volver
    Soda Stereo announces their comeback. They play in large stadiums in Argentina, Chile, Ecuador, Mexico, Colombia, Panama, Venezuela, the United States and Peru.There were 22 concerts and more than a million spectators, with fans from different generations.The group intends to stop again and set up a meeting in three years. Unfortunately, a tragic cardiovascular accident left Gustavo Cerati in a coma in May 2010, in the middle of his solo tour. He passed away in 2014.
